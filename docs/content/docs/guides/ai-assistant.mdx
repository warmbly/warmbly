The AI assistant is a chat panel built into the dashboard. It can look things up across your contacts, campaigns, mailboxes, unified inbox, CRM, team, and settings, and it can take almost any action you can take yourself, from editing a contact to sending a reply. It always asks before changing anything, sending is held to a stricter bar, and it can never do anything your own role could not, in your workspace and no other. Everything happens live: answers stream in word by word as they are generated, and each tool step appears the moment the assistant starts it.

Open it from the spark button in the top bar, or press `Cmd/Ctrl + I`. The panel stays with you as you move between pages, so you can keep a conversation going while you work.

Access is a role permission: members need **Use AI** (on by default for admins and managers, off for viewers) or the assistant and AI drafting are hidden entirely. Admins can also cap what each member spends per day, week, or month; see [AI credits](/guides/ai-credits/) and [Team and roles](/guides/team-roles/).

Conversations are private to the member who started them by default. A workspace admin can flip **Shared history** under **Settings, Workspace, AI assistant**: then every member with the Use AI permission sees every conversation in the history rail (attributed by owner) and can open, continue, or delete any of them. Turning it on exposes existing conversations to the whole team, so decide deliberately. Either way, delete a conversation from the history rail, or use **Clear history** at the bottom of the rail to remove every conversation you own in the workspace at once.

## Tabs, minimizing, and background runs

Conversations run as tabs, like an editor: open several at once with the **+** button, and a run keeps streaming in its own tab while you read another. The expand button turns the panel into a full workspace with your conversation history down the side, grouped by day and searchable; hover a conversation to delete it, which removes its transcript for good.

The panel is yours to arrange: drag its inner edge to resize, and move it to the left or right edge of the screen from the header (both remembered). On desktop it can also come off the edge entirely: click the pop-out button (or press `Alt + P`, or just drag the header away from the edge) and it becomes a floating window you can place anywhere and resize from any edge or corner. Its position and size are remembered, and the same button or shortcut docks it back. Inside the panel, `Ctrl/Cmd + ]` and `[` switch tabs, `Alt + N` opens a new chat, `Alt + W` closes the tab, and `Alt + M` minimizes; the full list is in the shortcuts dialog (`?`).

You do not have to keep the panel open to keep the assistant working:

- **Minimize** docks it into a compact status bar at the bottom of the screen. The bar shows what the assistant is doing live: working (with the step count), waiting for an approval, or holding a finished response you have not read. Click it to bring the panel back on that conversation.
- **Close it entirely** and any running work carries on. The spark button in the top bar shows a pulsing dot while a run streams, an amber dot when an action is waiting for your approval, and a solid dot when a response is ready; click it to reopen.

<Callout type="info" title="What it can and cannot do">
The assistant acts as you. It can only see and do what your own role allows, scoped to your workspace, so it can never reach data or actions you could not reach by hand, and never another organization's. Every change asks first, and sending a message is never auto-approved: you approve each send after seeing the full message.
</Callout>

## What you can ask

The assistant has a large set of tools it uses to answer you and act for you:

- Find, read, create, and delete contacts, edit their fields, tags, subscription, and notes, and bulk-edit many at once
- Read a contact's full activity timeline and the emails you have sent them
- List campaigns, read send/open/click/reply stats, and list a campaign's leads by status (queued, in progress, done with no reply, replied, bounced, unsubscribed), which is how it finds leads that went cold
- Create, edit, or delete a campaign, manage its sender mailboxes and tracking domain, read its logs, and add, edit, or remove its sequence steps
- Start or stop a campaign (always asks first)
- Read org-wide analytics, list your mailboxes, and manage a mailbox: change its sending limits and warmup, set a tracking domain, appeal a warmup ban, or disconnect it
- Read your unified inbox, draft a reply, or send a reply or a brand-new email for you (each send shows the full message and asks first)
- Snooze threads, set labels, mark threads read, and cancel a queued send
- Run your full CRM: deals, tasks, notes, pipelines, and stages, end to end
- Read and manage automations, including enabling one, which then runs on real events
- Manage your team (invite, change roles, remove), edit workspace and AI voice settings, read your plan and limits, and manage API keys and webhooks
- Search the web and read a public page

Because it knows what page you are on, you can say things like "summarize this campaign" or "draft a reply here" and it understands what "this" and "here" mean.

## How actions are approved

The assistant splits what it does into two kinds:

- **Reading** runs automatically. Looking up a contact or checking campaign stats needs no confirmation.
- **Changing** always asks first. When the assistant wants to update a contact, create a task, or set up a draft campaign, it pauses and shows you an approval card with a plain-language summary of exactly what it will do.

On an approval card you can:

- **Approve** to run just that action
- **Skip** to decline it and let the assistant carry on differently
- **Always allow** to let that kind of action run automatically from now on, for your whole workspace

Sending is different. An action that sends mail is never auto-approved, even if you have turned on **Always allow** for other actions. The approval card shows the full message and the mailbox it will send from, and it only sends when you approve that exact message. The assistant can send a reply into a thread or compose a brand-new email, and it refuses to send to an address your workspace has suppressed. Starting a campaign and enabling an automation are their own approvals too, since both can put mail in motion.

## Draft campaigns and automations

When you ask the assistant to build a campaign or an automation, it creates it as a **draft** (a campaign is never started, an automation is left disabled) and gives you a button to open it in the real editor. You review, adjust, and turn it on yourself. Nothing the assistant builds goes live on its own.

Any email copy or reply the assistant writes is grounded in your workspace [voice profile](/guides/unibox/#ai-reply-drafts) (what you sell, who you sell to, and your house voice, set under Settings, Workspace), and runs through the same human-writing rules as the rest of Warmbly, so it reads like a real person and not like AI.

## Credits and limits

Each step the assistant takes costs one AI credit, and a single request is capped at a set number of steps (shown as the run progresses) so a question can never run away. The credits remaining are shown at the bottom of the panel. If you run out, the assistant stops and tells you; see [AI credits](/guides/ai-credits/) for how allowances and top-ups work.

On a self-hosted install pointed at a free or local model, runs are not charged and the assistant shows a "free model" notice, since a smaller local model can produce lower-quality answers.

You can stop a run at any time with the stop button, and start a fresh conversation with the **+** tab button.

## Privacy

By default, conversations are private to you: other members of your workspace do not see your assistant sessions. If an admin turns on **Shared history** (Settings, Workspace, AI assistant), every conversation in the workspace becomes visible to and continuable by every member with the Use AI permission. Either way, when the assistant changes something (creates a task, updates a contact), that change shows up in the shared activity feed like any other edit, so your teammates' views stay current.
